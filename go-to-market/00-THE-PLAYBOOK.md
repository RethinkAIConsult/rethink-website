# RethinkAI Outbound Engine — The Operator's Pipeline Playbook

**One page, run it on Monday.** This is the finished system: positioning, targets, channels, honest math, the full copy library, a 30/60/90 rollout, and a weekly cadence you can actually sustain while delivering client work.

It is built around one truth and one constraint. The truth: **Jack's only un-fakeable edge is that he ran UK operations at Stratasys, makes operational claims no outsider can make, and knows the people — including the CEO. That edge converts at 35%; cold copy converts at 3-5%.** The constraint: **Jack is one person who must also deliver £30-40k/mo of client work, so the system has to keep producing pipeline the months he disappears into delivery.**

Everything below follows from those two facts. The cold lane is built to be legal, deliverable, and competent — table stakes, automated, low-craft. All the irreplaceable craft hours go where the edge is real and there is no ban risk: the warm graph, the ecosystem, the Zeif-pattern peer moves, and content. The cold machine feeds that; it is not the centre of gravity.

## 1. POSITIONING AND THE ONE-LINE WEDGE

Jack is not "an AI consultant who knows manufacturing." He is the only person reaching these buyers who ran UK operations at the company that built their printers, knows the CEO personally, and will tell them which AI projects *not* to fund. That is not a credential to list. It is the reason the message gets read.

**The wedge (the spine of everything):**

> I build production AI for manufacturing and operations-heavy companies — and I kill the projects that won't pay back. Ex-Stratasys, ran UK ops, no hype. I wrap the tools you already own; I don't sell you a platform.

**Three assets, ranked by rarity:**

1. **Operator, not engineer.** A COO can tell within two sentences whether someone has been inside the operation. Jack passes; competitors don't.
2. **Earned, demonstrated cynicism.** The market is exhausted by vendors selling transformation. The differentiator is not *claiming* to be honest (everyone does) — it's **naming the specific competitor pitch he'd tell them to refuse**: "Don't buy generative design or a smart-factory dashboard yet; at your volume neither pays back inside two years." Checkable insider behaviour beats "I'm the honest one."
3. **Production, not pilots.** 94% of AI deployments never reach production. Jack ships things that run (Oscilar, Alto/Zoopla). In a pilot-purgatory market the sharpest credential is "mine actually works."

**Handling rules:**

- **Stratasys is proof, not a signature.** Use it **once per sequence**, hard, in touch 1 or 3 — never both. Repeated across touches it becomes a verbal tic that devalues the one place it should hit like a hammer. Other touches earn operator credibility through *specificity* (a named machine class, a named workflow), not by restating the résumé line.
- **Lead with the observation, land Stratasys as the reframe:** "When I ran UK ops at Stratasys, the smart shops solved every ERP exception with a spreadsheet and someone's weekend. AI doesn't fix that by replacing the ERP — it sits on the gap."

## 2. ICP AND TARGET-LIST LOGIC

Three concentric rings. Effort is **inverted** from the obvious: the warm rings get the craft hours, the cold ring gets automation.

| Ring | Who | Why | Per-prospect reply | Effort posture |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ring 0 — Stratasys warm network** | Ex-colleagues, ex-resellers now inside manufacturers, former enterprise accounts, channel partners | 90% of first consulting contracts come from prior network. "Ex-Stratasys UK ops" = instant credibility. Markforged acquisition has every partner re-evaluating *now*. | ~35% | **Highest craft. One-time burn, done by hand, week 1.** |
| **Ring 1 — Additive / industrial spearhead** | Manufacturers running Stratasys/Markforged/Desktop Metal kit, 50-500 emp, ops/eng leaders; AM bureaux scaling into regulated (aero/defence/medical) | Inbox saturation ~10x lower than SaaS. Hooks un-fakeable. LinkedIn-led. | ~6% | High craft, LinkedIn only. |
| **Ring 2 — Horizontal ops base** | Insurance brokerage, 3PL, RIA, property mgmt, prof services. 50-300 emp, COO/CEO buyer, no in-house eng | The existing ~7,300 Apollo list. The volume layer. Email only. | ~3% | **Low craft, automated, table stakes.** |

**Ring 1 trigger filter (precision):** headcount growth 20%+ AND low engineering headcount AND a recent capital event (funded / PE-acquired / new tooling centre). **Live hook right now:** the **Stratasys → Markforged acquisition** — every reseller and customer is re-evaluating their stack; 90-day window; Jack is the only inbox who speaks to it from inside. Note the integration *pain* lands 6-12 months after the deal — so non-repliers today are the nurture list for Q4.

**Ring 2 list logic — the differentiation lever, used properly.** Do not build Ring 2 hooks as "5 contested claims about the vertical." Sector claims are horoscopes — true of everyone, so they read as mail-merge. Build Ring 2 as a **tech-stack-pair lookup**: enrich each prospect's actual stack (Apollo tech tags / BuiltWith / job posts), detect the gap between two named tools that have no native connector, and open by naming *that specific gap at their company*. This is the only thing that makes Ring 2 non-boring at volume — and it is simultaneously the only thing that defeats the spam-fingerprint problem (a genuinely unique opener, not a token-swap).

> Horoscope (template, deletes): "Every 3PL has the same hidden cost — the TMS tracks the shipment but not the 40 minutes someone spent keying the PO in."
> Specific (a human looked, gets a reply): "You're running {{TMS}} and {{accounting_system}} side by side. Those two don't have a native connector, so I'd bet someone on your team *is* the connector, re-keying POs by hand."

**Hard disqualifiers (run on every list, every time):** >10 engineers, building in-house AI, automation/IT-services *sellers* (supply side), active layoffs.

**The qualification rubric (what "qualified opp" actually means).** Pipeline is unfalsifiable without this. Score every booked call within 24h. Three+ green = qualified opp and counts toward pipeline; anything less is a *nurture*, not pipeline. Report qualified and junk pipeline separately so a soft month can't hide behind Stack-Review activity.

| Dimension | Disqualify if | Source of truth |
|---|---|---|
| **Buyer** | Not the economic buyer or one intro from them | Title + "who else signs off?" |
| **Pain named** | Can't name the hurting workflow in their own words | Their language |
| **Tools** | <2 systems that don't talk (nothing to wrap) | Discovery |
| **Capacity to build** | >10 engineers OR active in-house AI build | Apollo + the call |
| **Money** | No budget authority and no path to it in 90 days | "If this worked, where's the money?" |
| **Timing** | "Maybe next year" with no trigger | Trigger event |

**The Yoav Zeif move (deliberate, one-time, early).** One direct message to the Stratasys CEO — not a business ask, a peer observation from Jack's *new* vantage about where AM customers actually stall. The warmest possible activation; can open the entire partner/customer ecosystem. Strategic asset, used once, sent alone in week 1. (Copy in §5.)

## 3. THE CHANNELS AND HOW THE SYSTEM FITS TOGETHER

**Four lanes, each at its correct weight. The cold lane is the smallest, cleanest, most automated — not the centre.**

| Lane | Channel | Role | Craft posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warm graph (Ring 0)** | LinkedIn + email, by hand | Fastest cash, highest conversion. One-time burn. | Maximum craft. The real moat. |
| **AM spearhead (Ring 1)** | LinkedIn only (Sales Nav) | Differentiated pipeline. | High craft, low volume. |
| **Horizontal volume (Ring 2)** | Email only (cold domains) | Keeps the funnel full. | Low craft, automated, table stakes. |
| **Compounding** | LinkedIn content + referral loops | The multiplier that builds the £100k over months. | Batched once, dripped. Plus a referral ask baked into every delivery. |

**Why channels are split by ring, not run "all at correct weight":** a solo operator switching across coordinated profile-view → comment → connect → DM → email → voice-note collapses under the context-switching tax. So:

- **Ring 0/1 → LinkedIn.** Skip the comment warm-up *ritual* (it doesn't batch and is the first casualty of a busy week); send a one-line contextual connection note directly — the profile persuades.
- **Ring 2 → email.** It batches, automates follow-ups, and runs while Jack sleeps. **Never add a cold LinkedIn connect to a Ring 2 sequence** — that blows the weekly invite cap, tanks accept rate, and triggers "I don't know this person" reports, the fastest path to a restricted account.

**Deliverability and legal floor (existential, not a 2-hour setup task):**

- **Separate registered domains for cold** (e.g. `getrethinkai.com`, `rethinkai-team.com`), each with own mailboxes. **Never the brand root or its subdomain** — a torched subdomain bleeds onto `rethinkaiconsult.com` business mail. Set the brand domain DMARC to `p=reject` to protect it; the brand domain sends only warm/replied/inbound.
- SPF + DKIM + DMARC on every sending domain. Warm each mailbox 3-4 weeks (5/day → 25/day), keep warmup running permanently. 6-8 mailboxes across 2-3 cold domains for ~120-180/day. Cap 100/mailbox/day. Bounce <2%, spam complaints <0.1%.
- **Verify every list (MillionVerifier/NeverBounce) before the first send** — drop invalid/catch-all/risky. Apollo lists are stale; an unverified send on a fresh mailbox is the fastest route to a blacklist. This is a mandatory step in the Monday list-pull.
- **Plain text. No tracking pixels. No link in touches 1-2.** Pixels + a link on a cold first touch is a top spam trigger (and the reason to ignore open-rate is to *remove the pixel*, not just the metric).
- **UK GDPR / PECR — mandatory.** Every cold email carries a human-voiced opt-out as the last line: *"If this isn't relevant, just reply 'no' and I'll close the file — won't chase you."* (Doubles as a reply lift, satisfies the opt-out duty, looks human.) Maintain a real suppression list (VA owns it; "no/stop/not interested" suppresses across all mailboxes). Do a one-page legitimate-interest assessment once.
- **No LinkedIn automation tools.** Detection catches cross-tool patterns; a restricted Sales Nav account costs more than the time saved. Cap **total** new connection requests at ~80-100/week across all rings, tracked by the VA. **Withdraw unaccepted requests after 2-3 weeks** — a pile of pending invites lowers future accept rate.

**The nurture lane (the leak the old design ignored).** Sequences hard-stop at 3-4 touches, but the buying window is 6-18 months and the Markforged pain lands later. So nobody is deleted:

1. **"Not now" replies → tagged `nurture-warm`, re-entered at 90 days with a *fresh* take on a *new* event** — never "circling back."
2. **No-reply non-openers → the content lane nurtures them.** After a sequence ends, the VA adds them to a "seen-but-silent" Sales Nav list; Jack's comments are spent on *that list's* posts first. Dead cold contact → warm in-feed impression for £0.
3. **Monthly "one sharp thing" broadcast** to every non-converted contact who engaged — the month's best POV, one link, visible unsubscribe. Cheapest pipeline in the system.

## 4. THE £100k/MONTH PIPELINE MATH — HONEST, PER-PROSPECT

This is the section that tells the truth, because every prior version lied at two joints: a fantasy 45% positive-reply rate (real cold-outbound: ~15%) and a 350-prospect/month volume no delivering solo operator can personalise without fingerprinting. Corrected, here is what one pair of hands produces, and exactly what it takes to reach £100k.

**Definitions.** Reply rates are **per-prospect, whole-sequence** (not per-touch). "Positive reply" = will take a meeting — in real cold outbound that's **~15% of all replies**, not 45% (the rest are negative, wrong-person, or "send info"). Pipeline = expected contract value of a *qualified* opp (passed the §2 rubric). **Blended deal value: £12k** (a book weighted to £6-9k fixed-scope and 3-month retainers at ~£12k TCV blends here, not £16k).

**The corrected funnel (steady-state, new-ish sender, good copy):**

| Stage | Ring 1 | Ring 2 | Ring 0 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prospect → any reply | 6% | 3% | 35% |
| Replies that are positive | 15% | 15% | ~45% (warm) |
| Positive → meeting | 55% | 55% | 55% |
| Meeting → qualified opp | 45% | 45% | 45% |

So **positive → opp ≈ 0.55 × 0.45 ≈ 0.25.**

**Honest volume.** A solo operator who is *also delivering* sustains **~150 genuinely-personalised Ring 2 + ~30 Ring 1 prospects/month** — not 350. And sending is *bursty*: ~4 good weeks then a 3-week delivery blackout, so real annualised volume is ~55-60% of nominal. A VA pushes list *volume*; a VA cannot write the specific first line without collapsing reply rate to the boring baseline.

**Steady-state model (Ring 0 excluded — it's a one-time burn):**

| Source | Prospects/mo | Reply | Total replies | Positive (~15%) | Opps (×0.25) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ring 1 (AM, sharp) | 30 | 6% | 1.8 | 0.27 | 0.07 |
| Ring 2 (horizontal) | 150 | 3% | 4.5 | 0.68 | 0.17 |
| LinkedIn inbound (mo 6+) | — | — | 2.0 | ~0.8\* | 0.30 |
| Referral (post-delivery) | — | — | 1.5 | ~1.0\* | 0.30 |
| **Total (mature)** | | | **~10** | **~2.75** | **~0.85** |

\*Inbound and referral carry ~40-50% positive share because they're warm — which is exactly why they matter disproportionately and why the cold lane is *not* where the craft goes.

**The honest headline:**

- **Months 1-3 (ramp, Ring 0 firing):** Ring 0 is a **one-time ~£40-75k pipeline event** from ~60 warm contacts (60 × 35% reply × 45% positive × 0.25 ≈ 2-3 opps, *plus* the warm-only Teardown closes hot). This is the best ROI in the entire plan and it does not recur. **Months 1-3 spike toward £75-100k of pipeline. Real, and front-loaded.**
- **Steady-state solo (month 4+, Ring 0 spent, content not yet mature):** **~£15-25k/mo new qualified pipeline.** This is the honest floor for one delivering person.
- **Mature solo (month 7-12, content + referral compounding):** **~£25-40k/mo**, the two warm lines doing the heavy lifting.

**How £100k/mo *actually* gets built — name it plainly:** not by a solo cold engine. Two real paths, in order:

1. **The warm graph + content + referral, compounded.** This is where Jack is un-fakeable and where the math says the conversion is (35% vs 3%). Pour the craft hours here, not into Ring 2 volume.
2. **One trained appointment-setter (~£2-3k/mo + commission)** running the Ring 2 volume + enrichment lane, freeing Jack for Ring 1, Ring 0, Teardowns, and replies. With one SDR producing 400-500 *properly-enriched* prospects/mo at the corrected funnel, **£80-100k/mo pipeline becomes real in ~9-12 months. Solo, it does not.**

**The cash reality that reframes the goal.** Even £25k/mo of *pipeline* approaches what Jack can personally close and serve (4-6 client ceiling, ~£30-40k/mo revenue). So **for a solo operator, pipeline consistency beats pipeline volume.** A machine that reliably produces **2-3 qualified opps every month including delivery-sprint months** is worth far more than one that spikes to 6 then dies for two months and burns the network with a waitlist. The release valves that make pipeline convert to cash instead of a waitlist:

- **A productized fixed-scope offer — "One Workflow, Live in 3 Weeks", £6-9k** — converts overflow at margin without consuming a retainer slot.
- **A subcontractor bench (1-2 vetted builders)** so fixed-scope ships with Jack on architecture/QA only. **Stand this up *before* scaling outbound** — generating pipeline you can't serve is network arson.

**The single KPI the old design missed: cost-per-positive-reply in Jack-hours.** Ring 2 yields ~0.7 positive replies/mo from ~2.5 hrs/week ≈ 10 hrs/positive-reply — Jack's *worst* lane. Ring 0, referral, and inbound carry 3-4x the positive share. The math forces the strategy: **stop optimising Ring 2 volume; pour hours into referral loops and content.**

## 5. THE COPY LIBRARY

The voice: sharp ex-Stratasys operator, slightly cynical about AI hype, a real point of view, never boring. Plain English, no em-dashes, no corporate jargon.

**The two laws every message obeys (fixed per the critiques):**

1. **The opener must be a position they could disagree with** — and for Ring 2, anchored in *their specific stack*, not a sector horoscope.
2. **The close must do one of three things: dare them to correct you, attach a cost to silence, or flatter their competence into a brag-reply.** The banned close — *"Is that your situation, or am I off-base?"* — is the new "compare notes." It is a lint rule: no message ships with it.

Four message **shapes** (vary the *structure*, not just a rhetorical label, or the variant bank becomes one detectable fingerprint):
1. **Blunt, two sentences, no context** (changes length — breaks the fingerprint).
2. **Forwarded-fragment frame** ("saw this and thought of {{company}}: [one line]").
3. **A genuine org question with zero positioning** ("who owns the {{TMS}}-to-accounting reconciliation at {{company}} these days?") — gets replies precisely because it doesn't perform.
4. **Demonstrated-refusal** (name the competitor pitch you'd tell them *not* to buy).

### 5E. THE VALUE OFFER — TEARDOWN, STACK REVIEW, AND OFFER LADDER

```
Teardown (free, warm-only)  →  Stack Review (£0 cold / £1.5-2.5k warm)  →  Fixed-scope (£6-25k)  →  Retainer (£4k+/mo)
   the warm closer              the call's offer / paid entry              the cash engine            the recurring book
```

**The Teardown — GATED, warm-only.** A 5-7 min personalised Loom naming 2-3 automations Jack would build for that named company, with hours-back math and one first move. Honest cost: 45-90 min (templatize the reusable 50% per vertical so per-prospect work is 15-30 min). **It never fires cold at volume.** It fires only on: Ring 0, anyone who replied with interest, and a hand-picked few. **Ceiling 2/week**, first thing cut in a delivery-heavy week, **no 3-day SLA** ("I'll send it in the next few days" — never a deadline that collides with delivery).

The Teardown's trust device is mandatory: **the "what I would NOT build" line** — name the appealing thing he'd refuse because it won't pay back. That single move separates him from every transformation vendor.

Teardown script skeleton: earned open (prove you looked) → automation one (name it, locate the manual reality, size the hours) → automation two → **the honest cut (what he'd refuse and why)** → the one sequenced first move + soft close ("reply and I'll send a one-page scope; if I'm off, tell me where").

**The Stack Review — the deal-shaper / the call itself.** A productized 45-min live diagnostic producing a keepable artifact: *a ranked list of which workflows are worth automating this year and which aren't, with hours-back and cost-of-inaction in their numbers.* Free cold, £1,500-2,500 warm (filters seriousness, anchors value, credits against the build). **The "don't automate this" column is the entire differentiation** — every other vendor's diagnostic is a disguised sell sheet; Jack's tells them what *not* to spend money on, which is what makes a COO trust the rest of the page.

**The cash valves (load-bearing, stand up first):** the £6-9k "One Workflow, Live in 3 Weeks" fixed-scope product + a 1-2 person subcontractor bench so pipeline above the personal ceiling converts at margin.

**Teardown offer language (cold = deferred, warm = produced):**

- *Cold email (deferred, the reward for a pulse):* "If it's useful, I'll record you a 5-minute teardown of two automations I'd build at {{company}} and the hours they'd buy back — including the one thing I'd tell you *not* to build. No call needed to get it."
- *Warm DM (produced):* "I've been recording short teardowns for a few additive shops — 5 minutes, two automations I'd build at {{company}}, the math, and the one thing I'd tell you *not* to bother with. Want one? Say go and it's in your inbox this week."

### 5F. THE CALL — discovery-to-close spine (where the money converts)

The Stack Review *is* the call. A structured call converts 50%+; "let me tell you what I do" converts 15%. That gap is the whole £100k.

- **(0-3) Frame, don't chat.** "Here's how I'll use our 30: 20 minutes I ask how work actually moves through {{company}}, then I tell you straight which 1-2 things are worth automating this year and which aren't. You leave with the answer whether or not we work together. Fair?"
- **(3-20) Discovery on the gap — 5 scripted questions** mapped to the rubric: *Walk me through how a {{job/quote/shipment}} goes from start to done. Where does a human retype something? What breaks when volume doubles? Whose head holds the workaround? What have you already tried and why didn't it stick?* (The last surfaces the real objection before you pitch.)
- **(20-25) Play back the ranked list live + name what you'd NOT build.**
- **(25-28) Price on the call, anchored.** "First one's a fixed-scope build — One Workflow, Live in 3 Weeks, £7k. Or the ranked review written up first, £1,500, credits against the build." Never "I'll send a proposal."
- **(28-30) Close to a dated next action.** "Want me to send the one-page scope today and lock a start in the week of the {{X}}?"

### 5I. THE REPLY-HANDLER (the one thing that survives every mode)

A positive reply waiting three days converts at a fraction of one answered in an hour. **Mechanise it:** a phone push on any reply containing intent words ("yes/go/interested/send/how much/call"). Respond to *that ping*, not an inbox sweep. A 4-line bank makes it a 30-second send between meetings:

> "Brilliant — Tue 2pm or Wed 10am? Either way I'll come having already looked at {{company}}."
> "Yes — send me the two tools you live in and I'll have specifics ready."
> "Grab whatever works here, and tell me the one workflow bugging you most so I come prepared: {{link}}"

Speed-to-lead is a tracked KPI: median time-to-first-response to a hand-raise, target <2h in working hours. If it creeps past a day, *that's* the leak — not the copy.

## 7. WEEKLY OPERATING CADENCE AND KPIs

**Don't budget hours against an empty calendar — budget one protected block.** Outbound hours don't get *added* to a delivery week, they get *evicted* by it. So the floor is a single ring-fenced block; everything else is good-week upside.

**The protected block — one 2-hour slot, same time weekly (e.g. Friday 2-4pm), defended like a client meeting. In priority order:**
1. **Handle every reply** (calendar link to any positive reply on the spot).
2. **Send the week's banked sequences** (Ring 2 email batch — verified, mostly-automated follow-ups; 10-15 Ring 1 LinkedIn connects with a one-line banked note).
3. **If time remains:** one queued post goes live, or one owed Teardown gets recorded.

**Good-week additions (never load-bearing):** Monday research block (re-run Apollo, promote Ring 1 accounts, deep-research 5-8 Ring 1); the daily LinkedIn comment routine on the seen-but-silent list; voice notes; a second Teardown.

**Minimum-Viable Mode (delivery-sprint default ~30-40% of the time):** the protected block shrinks to reply-handling + the auto-running Ring 2 batch. No new research, no Teardowns, no content. ~2-3 hrs. **The hand-raise response never drops** — that's the one thing MVM protects.

**VA scope (from week 6, low-blast-radius only):** list pulls into a holding sheet (Jack reviews before load), list verification, scheduling, reminders, CRM hygiene, suppression-list ownership, withdrawing stale LinkedIn invites. Jack keeps the send button and merge-QA. Budget ~2 hrs/week management for the first month, out of the outbound budget.

**System of record (from day one, £0-20/mo):** track per-contact stage transitions, not just totals —
`Sourced → Sequenced → Replied → Positive → Booked → Showed → Qualified → Proposal → Won/Lost`
plus a fixed **lost-reason picklist** (price / timing / DIY-hire / built-in-house / no-pain). The conversion rate between *adjacent* stages is the diagnosis: low Sequenced→Replied = copy/deliverability; healthy Replied but low Positive→Booked = weak booking ask; healthy Booked but low Qualified = targeting; healthy Qualified but low Won = the call/close. Three top-line numbers can't tell these apart.

**KPIs — five, and only five:**

1. **Qualified meetings booked/week** (rubric-passed) — target 3-5; below 2 for two weeks → diagnose by stage, in order: deliverability, then list, then copy.
2. **Reply rate by variant** — kill <3% after 50 sends. Target Ring 1 6%+, Ring 2 3%+.
3. **Speed-to-lead** — median time to first response on a hand-raise, target <2h working hours.
4. **Pipeline velocity** — (qualified opps × close-rate × £12k) / cycle weeks; 8-week early warning.
5. **Cost-per-positive-reply in Jack-hours, by lane** — the strategic dial. When Ring 2 reads ~10 hrs/positive-reply and referral/inbound read 2-3x better, it's telling you to move hours out of cold volume and into the warm loops. **Ignore** open rate (no pixels anyway), SSI, total sends, impressions.

**Stack (under £200/mo):** cold-email sending tool + verification (MillionVerifier) + Sales Navigator (~£100) + Loom (free) + a £0-20 board (Notion/Airtable/Pipedrive). VA ~£400-600/mo from week 6. Add an appointment-setter (~£2-3k/mo) only on the §6 day-90 decision. Don't add Clay/Smartlead until volume exceeds 200/day.
